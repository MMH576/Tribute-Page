Walt Disney

The Architect of Imagination

Here is a brief timeline of Walt Disney's life:

  • 1901 - Born on December 5 in Chicago, Illinois.

  • 1911 - Moved with his family to Kansas City, Missouri.

  • 1917 - Dropped out of high school to join the army, but was rejected for being underage.

  • 1919 - Started his career as a commercial artist at the Pesmen-Rubin Commercial Art Studio.

  • 1920 - Co-founded the short-lived Iwerks-Disney Commercial Artists with Ub Iwerks.

  • 1922 - Founded Laugh-O-Gram Studio in Kansas City, which later went bankrupt.

  • 1923 - Moved to Hollywood and co-founded the Disney Brothers Studio with his brother Roy.

  • 1928 - Created Mickey Mouse, making his debut in "Steamboat Willie," one of the first sound cartoons.

  • 1932 - Won his first Academy Award for the creation of Mickey Mouse.

  • 1937 - Released the first full-length animated feature, "Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs."

  • 1940 - Opened the new Walt Disney Studios in Burbank, California.

  • 1950 - Released "Cinderella," which marked the studio's return to feature-length animated films after World War II.

  • 1955 - Opened Disneyland in Anaheim, California, revolutionizing the theme park industry.

  • 1961 - Established the California Institute of the Arts (CalArts), merging two existing institutions.

  • 1964 - Produced the film "Mary Poppins," which became a major success and won several Academy Awards.

  • 1965 - Announced plans for the Experimental Prototype Community of Tomorrow (EPCOT).

  • 1966 - Passed away on December 15 in Burbank, California, from lung cancer.
